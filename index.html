Registered Nurse

Whitney’s career in healthcare began in 2003 when she stepped into the operating room as a surgical technician. Over the years, she developed a deep passion for patient care and the fast-paced environment of surgery. After dedicating more than a decade to the operating room, she decided to advance her career by pursuing her Registered Nurse (RN) license. In 2017, she proudly graduated from an RN program, combining her hands-on surgical experience with her newfound nursing qualifications. This marked the beginning of her journey toward leadership in healthcare.

Master's in Healthcare Administration

When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, Whitney, like many others, faced the challenges of lockdowns and the strain the pandemic placed on the healthcare system. It was during this time that she made the pivotal decision to return to school, enrolling in a program to earn her Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree. Driven by her desire to make an even greater impact, she didn’t stop there. Whitney is currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Healthcare Administration, recognizing that broader knowledge in leadership and management would empower her to drive meaningful change in the healthcare industry.

Chief Nursing Officer

With her extensive experience in the operating room and her continued education, Whitney has set her sights on becoming a Chief Nursing Officer. Her goal is to apply her hands-on experience and administrative knowledge to create a healthcare environment that prioritizes both patient care and staff well-being. As mentioned in her interview, she believes in using her expertise to improve systems and advocate for the needs of healthcare professionals, ensuring that her teams have the resources and support they need to succeed. Whitney's journey reflects her dedication to not only advancing her own career but also making a lasting impact in the field of healthcare.
